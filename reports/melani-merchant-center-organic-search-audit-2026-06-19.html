Client audit - Merchant Center, Google Shopping, Organic Search

Melani Label: Organic Visibility and Product Feed Audit

This audit reviews when Melani's organic decline began, what changed across Merchant Center and Organic Search, and which fixes are most likely to improve traffic quality, click-through rate, and product discovery.

Prepared: 19 June 2026. External systems reviewed read-only. No Merchant Center, Shopify, feed-rule, API, or product-data changes made.

Title-rule deep dive

EvidenceThe suffix is applied across dresses, tops, skirts, pants, sets, and products with blank product type. This creates a quality issue because Google recommends titles that identify the exact product and put important details first.

Rule issueRows affectedWhy this mattersRecommended rule direction
Generic suffix on every title3,083Consumes visible title space with repeated account-level wording instead of product-specific query terms.Remove the account-wide suffix. Add product-specific terms only when truthful to that item.

Limitations

  • GA4 is the source of truth for revenue and purchases because Merchant Center purchase data appears absent or inconsistent before 2026.
  • Merchant Center Day exports are used for performance totals. Title exports are used for title-level distribution and may not reconcile exactly on impression totals.
  • Search Console historical availability was limited in this environment; do not treat pre-2026 Search Console trend coverage as complete unless separately exported.
  • Needs Attention diagnostics were not available as a supplied export. If Merchant Center shows no issues, retain screenshot evidence before stating the account has no current product issues.
  • No Merchant Center, Shopify, feed-rule, API registration, or product-data changes were made during this audit.

Google references

  • Merchant Center product performance metrics: Google defines impressions, clicks, CTR, purchases, and purchase rate; CTR is clicks divided by impressions.
  • Title attribute guidance: Google states titles should clearly identify the product, accurately describe the linked landing page product, distinguish variants, and use professional language.
  • Product data specification: Google uses product data to match products to queries; inaccurate or missing data can cause disapprovals, limited eligibility, incorrect display, and other issues.
  • Attribute rules: Google describes attribute rules as a way to transform product data, including concatenating attributes and static strings into titles.
  • Data sources: Google documents how data sources provide product information to Merchant Center.
  • Needs attention tab: Google describes issue views, impacted products, click potential, and downloadable issue views.
